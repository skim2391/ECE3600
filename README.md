New York
1. New York County doesn't have any regualtions, but New York state and city has ideas on regulations such as law for the building to be researched and reported before building it. There are lists of report 
2. Tompkins County haven't started regulated data centers, but Town of Dryen is trying to define the data center for local law.


California
1. Imperial County is a strong example of a county that is actively moving toward a formal data center regulatory framework through its draft Lithium Valley Specific Plan.
2. Santa Clara County itself does not yet show clear evidence of a new countywide data-center-specific regulatory push, but within the county, San José is actively shaping the data center landscape through project review, zoning/building administration, and utility coordination.

Illinois
1. Champaign County, Illinois is actively considering a 12-month moratorium on data center permits while a county task force develops new zoning and permitting standards for large data centers.
2. McLean County is clearly moving toward formal data center regulation, and the 2026 amendment shows a more mature and detailed regulatory approach than the 2025 version.
